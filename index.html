Contributions of Kathmandu Living Labs for Nepal's Reconstruction

National Housing Reconstruction Program

KLL signs the contract with UNOPS to assist Nepal Government collect data for National Housing Reconstruction Program. KLL develops an app called HRD Collect to be used by 1600 engineers in 14 earthquake affected districts. Using this mobile application, they are collecting building damage data, socio-economic data and demographic data of the affected regions.

More October 2015

Cultural Heritage Damage Assessment

KLL in collaboration with UNESCO and support of DoA (Department of Archaeology) mobilized volunteers to collect initial damage assessment data on cultural heritages in and around the Kathmandu Valley. A publicly accessible site is created and handed over to DoA.


More November 10, 2015

School Infrastructural Damage Assessment

The Department of Education and The World Bank asks KLL to support an assessment of damaged schools because of KLL’s expertise in mobile data collection technology. KLL develops a mobile data collection app KLL Collect SIDA. 2400 Civil engineers are deployed in the field in phase I of the project (Districts: Ramechhap, Kavrepalanchowk, Kathmandu, Lalitpur, Bhaktapur, Okhaldhunga, Makwanpur, Sindhuli). Phase I completes and phase II (Districts: Nuwakot, Rasuwa, Gorkha, Dhading, Sindhupalchok and Dolakha) starts from Dec 17, 2015.

November 16, 2015

Mapping Event for Global Shapers South Asia

Global Shapers Kathmandu hub approaches KLL to arrange a mapping event for their Global Shapers South Asia guests. Global Shapers South Asia is organized in Nepal for the first time and KLL is among the four hosts. KLL hosts 15 global shapers from 8 cities around South Asia and shares different experiences on its post-earthquake works. A mapping event is organized for them.

More November 21, 2015

Health parameters tracking of IDP Camps

KLL signs a contract with EpiNurse, a Japanese Government funded program that enables Japanese researchers collaborate with foreign nationals. KLL develops a mobile collection app which captures different health parameters of IDP camps. The app is capable of collecting data offline and the data becomes available to the central staff immediately upon submission to the central sever.

November 30, 2015

Tracking and Monitoring System Designed for Ncell

KLL works with Ncell to create tracking and monitoring system for the amount spent by the partners (Help Nepal Network and Clean Up Nepal) on relief and reconstruction by building temporary shelters, temporary toilets and permanent toilets and the impact on beneficiaries. KLL develops a mobile data collection app and a visualization for the data collected.

January 8,2016

Creating an Open Map Infrastructure in Pokhara

KLL signs a contract to lead the Secondary Cities project with American Association of Geographers in Pokhara. KLL will reflect and apply the learnings of the post-earthquake work of Kathmandu and prepare Pokhara for disasters in the field of data and information. A three day national level workshop “Learning from the April 25 Earthquake to Prepare for Future Disasters: Creating an Open Map Infrastructure in Pokhara” will be organized from March 14 – 16 that brought local people from diverse background together.

Feburary 28, 2016

Open Data Day celebrated at Pokhara for the first time

KLL co-organises Open Data Day in Pokhara in collaboration with GESAN, student club at Western Regional College and Research Management Unit, Tribhuwan University. There is talk sessions (Dr. Nama Raj Budhathoki, Dr. Swarnim Wagle and Hon. Mr. Rabindra Adhikari) followed by mapathon, data expedition and geocaching running in parallel. This one day event attracts around 300 participants. This is the first time Open Data Day was celebrated in Pokhara.
